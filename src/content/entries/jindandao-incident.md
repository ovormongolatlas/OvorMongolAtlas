The Jindandao (金丹道) incident was a Han secret-society revolt across the Josotu and Juu Uda leagues in November–December 1891 that massacred Mongols, suppressed by late December of that year. This atlas's master chronology records its lasting consequence as political rather than only immediate: it strengthened Mongol nationalism and prompted Beijing to route Mongol-Han disputes and tax collection through Chinese provincial authorities rather than the banners themselves, weakening banner autonomy going forward — the earliest node the chronology identifies in this atlas's [land-reclamation](/entry/land-reclamation) thematic thread. Every date and detail here is reference-tier only, not independently citable scholarship.

**Casualty figure UNSETTLED:** estimates across sources range so widely — roughly 150,000 to 500,000 — that this atlas treats the figure as genuinely contested rather than picking a number or a midpoint. This is recorded as a standing flag, not a gap to be filled by interpolation.
