This atlas's master chronology places Gungsangnorbu (Prince Gung, 貢桑諾爾布) at the start of several threads this atlas covers elsewhere in more built-out form — Kharachin Right Banner succession, early-1900s Mongol modernizing education, the Beijing Mongolian-Tibetan School that trained a later generation of Mongol nationalists, and a Japanese-leaning turn around the 1911 Xinhai Revolution — but every specific date and detail available to this atlas for him currently comes only from "reference/web — verify" tier sources: encyclopedic anchors, not independently citable scholarship. Per this atlas's sourcing rules, this entry is deliberately left as a stub rather than fleshed out with that material.

**Reference-tier dates, unsettled, not to be treated as fact:** birth given as either 1871 or 1872 (one account places the latter specifically at the Kharachin Right Banner palace); death given as either 1930 or 1931. This atlas's master chronology records this as an explicit, unresolved conflict (its Conflict D) and does not pick a side; the frontmatter years above (1871–1930) are the earliest-attested candidates, chosen only to satisfy this atlas's schema, not asserted as more likely correct than the alternatives.

**Scope, reference-tier only:** succeeded Prince [Vangdudnamjil](/entry/vangdudnamjil) as 12th and last jasag of Kharachin Right Banner, Josotu League, in 1898; founded one of Inner Mongolia's first modern schools (Chongzheng) in 1902; visited Meiji Japan in 1903 and founded a military school and a girls' school with Japanese teachers on his return; leaned royalist and pro-Japanese around the 1911–1913 Xinhai Revolution period; secured approval for the Mongolian-Tibetan School in Beijing in 1913, the institution this atlas's chronology identifies as the hinge between Gungsangnorbu's generation and the 1920s Mongol nationalist radicals (including the 1925 Inner Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party — see the [Inner Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party](/entry/inner-mongolian-peoples-revolutionary-party) stub entry).
