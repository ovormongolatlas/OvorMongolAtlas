The Mongolian Allied Autonomous Government succeeded the [Mongolian Allied League Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-league-autonomous-government) in September 1939 and continued under [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub)'s chairmanship until August 1941, when it was reorganized into the [Mongolian Autonomous State](/entry/mongolian-autonomous-state) (Jagchid).

This entry is thinner than the others in this succession. Jagchid's biography devotes a full chapter to the period, but for this atlas only that chapter's table of contents has been reviewed so far, not its narrative text — so what follows is a list of topics known to fall within this government's history rather than a connected account. Jagchid's chapter headings for the period cover: the regime's formation; the roles of Prince Sungjingwangchug and other senior officials; a secret, ultimately unsuccessful contact between Demchugdongrub and Chiang Kai-shek; the establishment of Wang Jingwei's Japanese-sponsored Nanjing regime and a related conference at Qingdao; a military clash at Wuyuan; a visit by Wang Jingwei to [Kalgan](/entry/kalgan); unrest in Ujumuchin; the founding of the Mongolian cooperatives (khorshiya); a second visit by Demchugdongrub to Japan; a visit to [Kalgan](/entry/kalgan) by Tojo Hideki; and the work of a Mongolian Rehabilitation Committee (Jagchid).
