# Mengjiang maps inventory (private, not for publication)

Catalog of the map files in `Mengjiang project/Photos/maps` on the Desktop, sorted by how each can be used. This file is a private research reference — it lives in the gitignored `research/` folder and should never be published on the site or copied into `src/`.

**Governing rule (per PROJECT_NOTES.md image-rights buckets):** tracing boundary, place-name, or railway data off a copyrighted map to build your own original GeoJSON is fine. Republishing the source map image itself — as a photo, scan, or crop — is not, unless its rights are separately cleared. Everything in sections 2 and 3 below is held under that rule.

Folder had 15 image/PSD files; all are accounted for below. A few didn't fit the three buckets you named, so I added a fourth (section 3, press/journalistic maps) rather than force them into the wrong category — flag if you'd rather I re-sort them.

## 1. Reference to trace boundaries from (data source only — do not republish the image)

- **English Nakamura (1941) map** — `english mengjiang map.png`. Clean modern redraw with a legend: state borders, league boundaries within Mengjiang, present-day state/province borders (dashed white), the Beijing–Baotou railway, and the Great Wall. Captioned "Source: Nakamura (1941)," cartography credited to Harald Krähe — so this is itself a modern secondary redraw of a 1941 Japanese source, not the 1941 original. Best single item for tracing league boundaries. Rights: unknown/likely in-copyright (modern cartographer's redraw); safe to trace from, not to republish.
- **Sven Hedin / Sino-Swedish Expedition sketch-map (1945)** — `TRV013M.jpg`. "Sketch-Map of Parts of Central and East Asia Traversed by the Sino-Swedish Expedition under the Leadership of Dr Sven Hedin," Sino-Swedish Expedition Reports Publication 26, A.-B. Kartografiska Institutet, Esselte AB, Stockholm, 1945. Covers a huge swath from East Turkistan to Kalgan; explicitly labels "Barun Sunit" and "Residence of Barun Sunit Wang" on the eastern panel — directly useful for West Sünit geography. Rights: in-copyright (Swedish institutional publication, 1945).
- **Lattimore-era banner/ethnographic map** — `mapofmongols1930s.png`. Detailed English-language map of Inner and Outer Mongolia with named banners and tribal groups (Torgut, Durbet, Ujumuchin, the numbered Sunid-area banners, Ordos, Alashan, etc.), scanned from a book (page furniture visible at the crop edges). Not yet confirmed which book/edition. Rights: unknown (scanned book page, presumed in-copyright pending identification).
- **Simplified English schematic (unconfirmed source)** — `Screenshot 2026-06-22 at 10.35.59 PM.png`. Low-res black-and-white outline map: Soviet Russia / Outer Mongolia / Manchukuo / China, with "Inner Mongolia" and "Chahar" shaded as distinct regions. Much coarser than the Lattimore map above but still a usable rough-boundary reference. Source publication not identified from the crop. Rights: unknown, presumed in-copyright.

## 2. Propaganda / self-representation artifacts (hold — unknown provenance, do not publish until cleared)

These show the regime's own territorial claim or self-image, not necessarily actual control, and not necessarily accurate.

- **蒙疆地域圖 ("Map of the Mengjiang region")** — `map of mengjiang.jpeg`. Chinese-titled wartime map with administrative zones highlighted in red and a red rail/route line running north–south. No visible publisher or date in the scan. Rights: unknown, presumed in-copyright.
- **蒙疆全圖 ("Complete map of Mengjiang")** — `map of mengjiang 2 .jpeg`. Photographed paper map (shot at a slight angle, visible paper texture/fold), with a compass rose and a 1:600,000 scale bar, place names in Chinese. No visible publisher or date. Rights: unknown, presumed in-copyright.
- **Pictorial map with the Mengjiang flag** — `mapofmengjiang3.jpg`. Illustrated, colorized map with vignettes of camels, horses, and figures in regional dress scattered across the territory, and the Mengjiang flag (the red/white/blue/yellow horizontal-stripe design already sourced to Jagchid, p. 219, in the Mengjiang entry) reproduced prominently in the upper right. This is the clearest "self-image" artifact of the set — decorative, promotional, undated. Rights: unknown, presumed in-copyright; treat as highest sensitivity given how clearly promotional it is.
- **North China–Mengjiang Railway map (北支蒙疆鐵道略圖)** — `north china and mengjaing railway map.jpg`. Large color rail-network poster issued by the North China Transportation Company (華北交通株式會社), explicitly dated Showa 14 (1939) in the map's own text. Includes a small inset political map ("鐵道連絡圖," bottom left) showing the Mengjiang territory in color alongside Manchukuo, Japan, and Korea. Rights: in-copyright (Japanese wartime corporate publication).
- **Close detail of the same or a companion colored political map** — `Screenshot 2026-01-25 at 12.13.21 AM.png`. A tight, higher-resolution crop showing the Mengjiang region in tan/orange, bordering Outer Mongolia, Ningxia, and a green Manchukuo, with place names (Zhangjiakou/Kalgan, Beijing, Datong, Baotou, Chengde, Shanhaiguan, etc.). Color scheme and geography strongly match the railway map's inset above — very likely a zoomed detail of that same inset, possibly from a higher-res copy. Treat as the same source/rights as the item above unless you confirm otherwise.
- **內蒙古地圖 ("Inner Mongolia map"), low-res scan** — `controlled territory of mengjiang.jpg`. Small (640×396), low-quality monochrome halftone scan; your own filename suggests you collected it to show the regime's claimed/controlled extent. No publisher or date visible. Rights: unknown, presumed in-copyright.
- **內蒙古地圖, companion/duplicate scan** — `mo00.jpg`. Same title and layout as the item directly above (halftone dot pattern, same general composition) — likely the same source map from a different scan generation or crop. Rights: unknown, presumed in-copyright.
