The Mongolian Military Government (Монголын цэргийн засгийн газар) was the first Mongol governing body formed with direct Japanese sponsorship, replacing the weaker [Mongolian Political Affairs Council](/entry/mongolian-political-affairs-council). It followed two years in which Kwantung Army intelligence officers — including Doihara Kenji (November 1934) and Itagaki Seishiro (September 1935), both later convicted as Class A war criminals — had courted [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub) directly (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese").

A "Mongolian Army General Headquarters" was inaugurated at Demchugdongrub's own palace on February 12, 1936, using the Chinggis Khan calendar and a new blue banner rather than Republic of China symbols. Around this time, per Zorigt, the Kwantung Army executed four Khingan-area Mongol leaders — including Pingshen, described as the chief of the northern Khingan province — on March 26, 1936, on charges of conspiring toward Mongol unification and independence (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese"). Independent contemporary confirmation of roughly this same episode comes from the *New York Times*, which reported around May 1, 1936 that Japanese executions of four Hsingan Mongol leaders had estranged Demchugdongrub from Japan, and that the Nationalist government in Nanjing had separately ordered him to dissolve his autonomy council and leave Pailingmiao ("Inner Mongolia Wavers"). Contemporary observers speculated he might turn instead toward the Soviet Union and Outer Mongolia — a genuine open question at that moment, not a settled realignment, and one that events soon overtook ("Inner Mongolia Wavers").

A first Mongolian Congress followed at Ujumuchin on April 24, 1936, where delegates debated a full Mongol state spanning Inner and Outer Mongolia and Kokonor before settling on the more modest Mongolian Military Government, formally established at Dehua (Huade) on May 12, 1936, with Demchugdongrub at its head (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese"; Jagchid).

A Mongol attempt to seize Suiyuan from Chinese forces that November ended in defeat at Bat-khaalag itself (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese"). The government dissolved after full-scale war broke out between China and Japan in July 1937; Demchugdongrub's forces took Guisui ([Hohhot](/entry/hohhot)) that October, and a second Mongolian Congress there created its successor, the [Mongolian Allied League Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-league-autonomous-government).
