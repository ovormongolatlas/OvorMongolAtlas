The North Shansi Autonomous Government (晋北自治政府) was established at Tatung (present-day Datong, Shanxi) on October 15, 1937, weeks after the [South Chahar Autonomous Government](/entry/south-chahar-autonomous-government) and in the same wave of Japanese military expansion that took [Hohhot](/entry/hohhot) that same month (Boyle). Like its South Chahar counterpart, it was a Chinese, not Mongol, puppet administration, distinct from [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub)'s own Mongol government.

This atlas's master chronology independently corroborates the October 15, 1937 date and Tatung seat via Hirokawa 2024. The [United Committee of Mengchiang](/entry/united-committee-of-mengchiang), established November 22, 1937, unified this government with the South Chahar Autonomous Government and Demchugdongrub's Mongol government under a single Japanese-controlled coordinating body — see that entry for the fuller genealogy. Its head, per Jagchid's already-established account of the September 1, 1939 merger (recorded in the [Mongolian Allied Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-autonomous-government) entry), was Xia Gong, who became a deputy chairman of the merged government at that later date. That same entry records that at the start of 1943 the North Shanxi and South Chahar governments — by then long since demoted to political bureaus, not independent regimes — were abolished outright and converted into ordinary provinces (per the [Mengjiang](/entry/mengjiang) entry, citing Lückenhaus).

This atlas holds no further detail on this government's internal administration, personnel beyond Xia Gong, or territorial extent within North Shanxi; a fuller entry would need dedicated sourcing this atlas does not currently have.
