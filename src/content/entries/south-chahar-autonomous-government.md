The South Chahar Autonomous Government (察南自治政府) was established at [Kalgan](/entry/kalgan) on September 4, 1937, in the opening weeks of full-scale war between China and Japan, as part of the same Japanese military push into Chahar province that took Suiyuan the following month (Boyle). It was a Chinese, not Mongol, puppet administration — distinct from [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub)'s own [Mongolian Military Government](/entry/mongolian-military-government), which continued to operate alongside it in the same period.

This atlas's master chronology independently corroborates the September 4, 1937 date and Kalgan seat via Hirokawa 2024. Within weeks the [United Committee of Mengchiang](/entry/united-committee-of-mengchiang), established November 22, 1937, unified this government with the [North Shansi Autonomous Government](/entry/north-shansi-autonomous-government) and Demchugdongrub's Mongol government under a single Japanese-controlled coordinating body — see that entry for the fuller genealogy. Its head, per Jagchid's already-established account of the September 1, 1939 merger (recorded in the [Mongolian Allied Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-autonomous-government) entry), was Yu Pinqin, who became a deputy chairman of the merged government at that later date.

This atlas holds no further detail on this government's internal administration, personnel beyond Yu Pinqin, or territorial extent within Chahar; a fuller entry would need dedicated sourcing this atlas does not currently have.
