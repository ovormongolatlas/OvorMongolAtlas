The Mongolian Autonomous Government was [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub)'s last attempt at Mongol self-rule, proclaimed at Dingyuanying (present-day Bayanhot) in the Alashan Banner in August 1949 — four years after the collapse of [Mengjiang](/entry/mengjiang) and his subsequent house arrest in Beijing until 1949, when the Nationalist government's weakening position led it to court Mongol leaders again (Dudin). It is also referred to in later scholarship as the Alashan Republic or, after a September 1949 rename by the faction that stayed behind, the Western Mongolian Autonomous Government (Jagchid; Dudin).

## Founding

Demchugdongrub arrived in the northwest in early 1949 with Nationalist government approval, meeting Chinese military and political leaders in Xi'an and Lanzhou before being welcomed to Dingyuanying by Damzana, the jasag prince of Alashan — also transliterated in some sources as Dali Zhai or Dalizhaya, the same individual under different romanization (Jagchid; Dudin). An Inner Mongolian League and Banner Delegate conference on April 13, 1949 established a Preparatory Committee for Inner Mongolian Autonomy, with Bayankhan (Ba Wenjun) as chairman; Jagchid, who took part directly as a drafter and delegate, records that Demchugdongrub deliberately avoided the committee's chairmanship to avoid the appearance that he personally instigated every autonomy movement (Jagchid).

Demchugdongrub then traveled to Guangzhou, where the Nationalist government under Li Zongren was based, seeking formal recognition and material support. The trip yielded little beyond nominal approval of the Preparatory Committee and a token 10,000 silver dollars and some handguns and rifles — Jagchid describes the central government's assistance as "limited to what could be stuffed into his small airplane" (Jagchid).

A Mongolian People's Delegate Conference convened at Dingyuanying on August 5, 1949, passing a Law of Mongolian Autonomy and a Declaration of Mongolian Autonomy modeled on Sun Yat-sen's teachings on minority self-determination. On August 8, delegates elected Demchugdongrub chairman by secret ballot (160 of 175 votes) and Damzana vice chairman (174 votes); Wu Heling was elected speaker of a 31-member Mongolian Congress, with Chen Nasanbatu (Cheng Nasunbat) as vice speaker — a detail corroborated independently by both Jagchid's account and Dudin's (Jagchid; Dudin). The government was formally inaugurated on August 10, 1949.

## Structure and collapse

The government's cabinet included Bayankhan as secretary-general, He Zhaolin heading the Bureau of Internal Affairs, Bai Haifeng heading the Bureau of Industry, and a Peace Preservation Committee under Damzana with Li Shouxin and Bai Haifeng as deputies (Jagchid). Dudin's account, drawing on Mongolian-language sources not used here directly, gives a fuller cabinet list and notes the new government's flag largely reproduced the 1937–1939 Mengjiang flag (Dudin).

Within weeks the government's position collapsed alongside the wider Nationalist retreat: Xi'an and then Lanzhou fell to Communist forces in August 1949, and the Alashan leadership split over how to respond. Demchugdongrub wanted to move into the desert and, if necessary, on to Tibet and India to form a government in exile; Damzana's faction, backed by Bai Haifeng and He Zhaolin, argued for staying and negotiating a surrender (Jagchid). On September 20, 1949, Demchugdongrub left Dingyuanying with the government's official seal and name plate, moving west with a body of troops under Li Shouxin. The faction left behind — Damzana, Bai Haifeng, He Zhaolin, and Bayankhan — renamed their rump administration the Western Mongolian Autonomous Government and surrendered to Communist forces at Ningxia city on October 5, 1949 (Jagchid). Dudin's account gives the same date for the surrender and notes that presidential authority formally passed to Damzana (as vice president) on October 1, 1949 (Dudin).

Demchugdongrub continued moving west with his remaining followers before ultimately crossing into the Mongolian People's Republic; see the [Demchugdongrub entry](/entry/demchugdongrub) for his arrest there in February 1950 and the rest of his life. Most of his followers who had moved west with him surrendered to Communist forces in early 1950 under terms brokered by Ulanhu, the chairman of the Inner Mongolian Autonomous Region (Jagchid).
