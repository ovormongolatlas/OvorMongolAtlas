Gada Meiren (personal name Nadmid; a *meiren*, banner militia officer) led resistance, then armed uprising, after the Fengtien warlord administration under Zhang Xueliang colluded with banner authorities in 1929 to sell or reclaim roughly three-quarters of Darhan Banner's grassland (Khorchin Left Wing Middle Banner, Jirim League) to Han settlers — see the [land-reclamation](/entry/land-reclamation) theme entry for this episode's place in that atlas-wide thread. He was surrounded and killed at the Shar Mörön (Xar Moron) river around April 4–5, 1931, by a Fengtien force this atlas's master chronology names as led by [Li Shouxin](/entry/li-shouxin) (Buyandelger); the uprising dispersed after his death. Every date and specific here is reference-tier — not independently citable scholarship — except the Li Shouxin identification, which is now recorded in Li Shouxin's own entry, tagged SINGLE-SOURCE PERPETRATOR ACCOUNT, since it rests on Li's own 1985 memoir as relayed via Narangerel Part 2; see that entry for the fuller (though still limited) treatment this atlas already gives that specific connection.

**Folk memory vs. documented fact:** a folk song and a 1979 narrative poem (composed roughly in the 1950s) commemorating Gada Meiren both cast the warlord Zhang Zuolin as villain — but Zhang Zuolin was assassinated in 1928, before the Darhan land sale; the actual backer was his son Zhang Xueliang's Fengtien regime. Some lower-tier sources also wrongly identify the settlers as Japanese. This atlas's master chronology records this as an explicit, unresolved conflict between folk memory and documented fact (see the land-reclamation entry), and does not resolve it here either.

**VERIFY-GATE:** Borjigin Burensain, 遼北荒 [a reclamation-and-uprising study], 2003 — named in this atlas's master chronology as the authoritative source for the uprising itself (operational history: dates, troop numbers, chain of command, the uprising's course beyond its beginning and end). This atlas does not hold this source. A separate strand — Li Shouxin's own role — additionally requires Narangerel's article, Part 2 (not independently held this pass; see the Li Shouxin entry's own sourcing note), strictly tagged SINGLE-SOURCE PERPETRATOR ACCOUNT wherever used, since it is the suppressing commander's own late-life account. Do not expand this entry's account of the uprising itself until Borjigin Burensain has been consulted.
