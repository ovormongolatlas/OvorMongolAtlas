The United Committee of Mengchiang (蒙疆聯合委員会, Japanese Mökyō Rengō Iinkai) was established at [Kalgan](/entry/kalgan) on November 22, 1937, as a Japanese-controlled coordinating body over three separate regimes: [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub)'s Mongol government (by then the [Mongolian Allied League Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-league-autonomous-government), just inaugurated at Hohhot), the [South Chahar Autonomous Government](/entry/south-chahar-autonomous-government) at Kalgan, and the [North Shansi Autonomous Government](/entry/north-shansi-autonomous-government) at Tatung (Boyle, p.132–133; Hirokawa 2024, via this atlas's master chronology). It fits the pattern of leashed, counterweight-style Japanese sponsorship the [Mengjiang](/entry/mengjiang) entry describes at more length — folding a Mongol autonomy government together with two Chinese puppet regimes under one Japanese-run committee, rather than letting any of the three stand alone.

## Same body, four names

This atlas already recorded, before this entry existed, two other names for what is very likely this same body on the same date: [Sechin Jagchid](/entry/sechin-jagchid) dates the emergence of the name "Mengjiang" specifically to a Japanese-run "Joint Committee of Mongolian Territories," formed November 22, 1937 (see the Mengjiang and Mongolian Allied League Autonomous Government entries); and Tuo, independently, records a "Mengjiang Federation Committee" forming at Kalgan on the same date to unite the same three regimes (see the Mengjiang entry's Tuo-sourced chronology). Boyle's "United Committee of Mengchiang" and Hirokawa's 蒙疆聯合委員会 / Mökyō Rengō Iinkai now make four independent-source names — Jagchid, Tuo, Boyle, Hirokawa — all converging on November 22, 1937 for what appears to be one body. No single source among these four states the equivalence outright; this atlas treats the convergent dating as high-confidence, not certain, and this entry is the vehicle for holding that identification rather than leaving it scattered across other entries' cross-reference notes.

[Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub) resigned in April 1939 as Director-General of this body, in dissatisfaction with Japanese policy; the Japanese Supreme Adviser [Kanai Shōji](/entry/kanai-shoji) took over (Boyle, p.133) — see the Demchugdongrub entry's existing "Boyle's account" section, where this claim was recorded before this entry existed as an open question about which body Boyle meant; that question is now substantially resolved by the identification above. The Sept 1, 1939 founding ceremony of the merged [Mongolian Allied Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-autonomous-government) took place, per Jagchid, "in the courtyard of the Joint Committee of Mongolian Territory" — this atlas's own established account of the same physical body this entry describes, still under Jagchid's own name for it.

## Dissolution

This body's own institutional end is not separately dated in any source this atlas holds; it was superseded in practical terms when the three regimes it coordinated merged outright into the single Mongol United Autonomous Government (Mengjiang) on September 1, 1939, per the sources already cited in the Mongolian Allied Autonomous Government entry. This entry's endYear follows that merger date as the point at which a separate coordinating committee over three nominally distinct regimes ceased to have a function, rather than any source's stated dissolution date.
