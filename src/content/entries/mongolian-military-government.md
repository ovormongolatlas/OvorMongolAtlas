The Mongolian Military Government (Монголын цэргийн засгийн газар) was the first Mongol governing body formed with direct Japanese sponsorship, replacing the weaker [Mongolian Political Affairs Council](/entry/mongolian-political-affairs-council). It followed two years in which Kwantung Army intelligence officers — including Doihara Kenji (November 1934) and Itagaki Seishiro (September 1935), both later convicted as Class A war criminals — had courted [Demchugdongrub](/entry/demchugdongrub) directly (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese").

A "Mongolian Army General Headquarters" was inaugurated at Demchugdongrub's own palace on February 12, 1936, using the Chinggis Khan calendar and a new blue banner rather than Republic of China symbols. A first Mongolian Congress followed at Ujumuchin on April 24, 1936, where delegates debated a full Mongol state spanning Inner and Outer Mongolia and Kokonor before settling on the more modest Mongolian Military Government, formally established at Dehua (Huade) on May 12, 1936, with Demchugdongrub at its head (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese"; Jagchid).

A Mongol attempt to seize Suiyuan from Chinese forces that November ended in defeat at Bat-khaalag itself (Zorigt, "Cooperation with the Japanese"). The government dissolved after full-scale war broke out between China and Japan in July 1937; Demchugdongrub's forces took Guisui (Hohhot) that October, and a second Mongolian Congress there created its successor, the [Mongolian Allied League Autonomous Government](/entry/mongolian-allied-league-autonomous-government).
